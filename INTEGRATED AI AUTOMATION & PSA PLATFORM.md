`ROOT CAUSE #1: PROCESS MISIDENTIFICATION`

`The #1 killer of RPA projects: automating the wrong process.`

`Companies often automate processes that look amenable to RPA`   
`but actually require judgment, handle exceptions frequently,`

`✓ Rules-based (not judgment-heavy)`  
`✓ Structured data (not unstructured documents)`  
`✓ <10% exception rate`  
`✓ Stable interfaces (not frequently changing UIs)`  
`✓ High volume (100+ monthly transactions)`  
`✓ Measurable inputs/outputs`  
`✓ Low regulatory risk`

`Only automate processes that clear ALL 7 gates.`

`Most RPA implementations budget 0–5% of initial project cost`

`Reality: 20–30% of realized savings gets consumed by bot`   
`maintenance.`

`Why? Because every system change breaks bots:`  
`• ERP UI update → bot crashes`  
`• Banking portal redesign → login logic fails`  
`• Tax code change → calculation logic needs revision`  
`• Payer form update → extraction rules invalid`

`The industry calls this the "configure once, maintain forever"`   
`problem. It kills 25% of legacy RPA projects.`

`Our Fix: Built-in Maintenance Reserves`  
`We price implementations assuming 15–20% of initial cost`   
`annually for maintenance. This creates:`  
`• Budget buffer for unexpected changes`  
`• Realistic client expectations`  
`• Foundation for managed services revenue`

`───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────`

`ROOT CAUSE #3: BRITTLENESS TO EXCEPTIONS`

`Traditional RPA bots execute predetermined paths. They fail`   
`when exceptions occur.`

`Example:`   
`• Invoice processing bot expects 5 line items.`   
`• One invoice has 12 items (bulk order).`   
`• Bot crashes or delivers incorrect output.`

`30–50% of initial RPA projects fail because of this brittleness.`

`Our Fix: Agentic AI Layer`  
`Instead of rules-based bots, we layer agentic AI that:`  
`• "Understands" intent (not just following rules)`  
`• Handles exceptions intelligently (AI classifies, re-routes)`  
`• Self-heals when systems change (ML models adapt)`  
`• Learns from feedback (continuous improvement)`

`Result: 70%+ automation rate (vs. 40–50% for pure RPA).`
